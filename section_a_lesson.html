📚 Key Vocabulary — Chapter 3, Section A

Learn these terms before we dive in! Hover over each card to explore.

Equation
A mathematical statement that shows two expressions are equal, connected by an = sign.
Example: x + 5 = 12  |  3n = 24
Variable
A letter that represents an unknown number we are trying to find.
Example: In x + 3 = 9, the variable is x
Solution
The value of the variable that makes the equation true.
Example: The solution to x + 5 = 12 is x = 7
Inverse Operations
Opposite operations that undo each other. Used to isolate the variable.
Addition ↔ Subtraction  |  Multiplication ↔ Division
Isolate the Variable
To get the variable alone on one side of the equation so we can see its value.
x + 4 = 10 → subtract 4 → x = 6
Properties of Equality
Rules stating you can do the same operation to both sides of an equation without changing its truth.
If x + 3 = 10, then x + 3 − 3 = 10 − 3
Coefficient
The number multiplied by a variable.
In 5x, the coefficient is 5
Check (Verify)
Substituting your answer back into the original equation to confirm it is correct.
If x = 7: 7 + 5 = 12 ✓

📐 3.1 — Solving Equations by Adding or Subtracting

An equation is like a perfectly balanced scale. Whatever you do to one side, you MUST do to the other side to keep it balanced!

x + 5 = 12   means    "some number plus 5 equals 12"

🔑 The Big Idea: Use the Inverse (Opposite) Operation

To solve an equation with addition → use subtraction
To solve an equation with subtraction → use addition

Always do the same thing to both sides of the equation.

📋 Step-by-Step: Solving with Subtraction

Solve:   x + 8 = 15
  • 1
    Identify the operation: Addition (+8 is added to x)
  • 2
    Use the inverse: Subtract 8 from BOTH sides
    x + 8 − 8 = 15 − 8
  • 3
    Simplify: x = 7
  • 4
    Check: 7 + 8 = 15 ✓   Correct!

📋 Step-by-Step: Solving with Addition

Solve:   n − 6 = 10
  • 1
    Identify the operation: Subtraction (6 is subtracted from n)
  • 2
    Use the inverse: Add 6 to BOTH sides
    n − 6 + 6 = 10 + 6
  • 3
    Simplify: n = 16
  • 4
    Check: 16 − 6 = 10 ✓   Correct!

🌍 Real-Life Example #1 — Shopping Trip

Terria went shopping. After spending $13 at the bookstore, she had $27 left. How much money did she start with?

Let m = money she started with.
Equation: m − 13 = 27
Solution: Add 13 to both sides → m = 40
She started with $40. Check: 40 − 13 = 27 ✓

🌍 Real-Life Example #2 — Temperature

The temperature rose 9°F during the day and reached 74°F. What was the morning temperature?

Let t = morning temperature.
Equation: t + 9 = 74
Solution: Subtract 9 from both sides → t = 65
Morning temperature was 65°F. Check: 65 + 9 = 74 ✓

✖️ 3.2 — Solving Equations by Multiplying or Dividing

Now we tackle equations where the variable is being multiplied or divided. The rule is the same: use the inverse operation on both sides!

5x = 35   means   "5 times some number equals 35"

🔑 The Big Idea

To solve with multiplication → use division
To solve with division → use multiplication

If  ax = b  →  divide both sides by a:   x = b ÷ a
If  x/a = b  →  multiply both sides by a:   x = b × a

📋 Step-by-Step: Solving with Division

Solve:   4x = 28
  • 1
    Identify the operation: Multiplication (4 times x)
  • 2
    Use the inverse: Divide BOTH sides by 4
    4x ÷ 4 = 28 ÷ 4
  • 3
    Simplify: x = 7
  • 4
    Check: 4 × 7 = 28 ✓   Correct!

📋 Step-by-Step: Solving with Multiplication

Solve:   x ÷ 6 = 9
  • 1
    Identify the operation: Division (x divided by 6)
  • 2
    Use the inverse: Multiply BOTH sides by 6
    x ÷ 6 × 6 = 9 × 6
  • 3
    Simplify: x = 54
  • 4
    Check: 54 ÷ 6 = 9 ✓   Correct!

🌍 Real-Life Example #1 — Sharing Pizza

A group of friends shared a pizza equally. Each person got 3 slices, and there were 5 friends. How many slices were in the pizza?

Let s = total slices.
Equation: s ÷ 5 = 3
Solution: Multiply both sides by 5 → s = 15
The pizza had 15 slices. Check: 15 ÷ 5 = 3 ✓

🌍 Real-Life Example #2 — Earning Money

Terria earns the same amount each hour babysitting. After 4 hours, she earned $48. How much does she earn per hour?

Let h = hourly rate.
Equation: 4h = 48
Solution: Divide both sides by 4 → h = 12
She earns $12 per hour. Check: 4 × 12 = 48 ✓
