--��️ Literary Device: Call to Action
Definition: A literary technique where the author directly urges the reader to DO something ------ to change, act, or think differently.
""You now know the history. You know how racist ideas were born. You know they were created, not discovered. So: what are you going to DO about it?""
💡 Why Reynolds uses this: Reynolds ends the entire book with a call to action aimed at YOU ------ because knowing the history is only the beginning. The next step is yours.

📝 Grammar Focus: CER Writing: Claim, Evidence, Reasoning
In argumentative writing: CLAIM = your position. EVIDENCE = facts that support it. REASONING = WHY the evidence proves your claim.
Example: Claim: Systemic racism still exists. Evidence: Black Americans are incarcerated at 5x the rate of white Americans for similar offenses. Reasoning: This disparity reflects bias in the system, not differences in behavior.
