📚 Key Vocabulary — Section B

Two-Step Equation
An equation that requires two operations to isolate the variable.
2x + 3 = 11 (undo +3, then undo ×2)
Order of Operations (Reverse)
When solving, undo operations in reverse PEMDAS order — addition/subtraction first, then multiplication/division.
In 3x + 5 = 20: subtract 5 first, then divide by 3
Like Terms
Terms that have the same variable and exponent. They can be combined by adding/subtracting coefficients.
3x and 5x are like terms → 3x + 5x = 8x
Combining Like Terms
Adding or subtracting like terms to simplify an expression before solving.
4x + 2x − 1 = 17 → 6x − 1 = 17
Distributive Property
Multiplying a number outside parentheses by each term inside: a(b + c) = ab + ac
3(x + 4) = 3x + 12
Simplify
To combine like terms and apply the distributive property to make an expression as simple as possible.
2x + 3x + 7 = 5x + 7
Constant
A fixed number with no variable attached.
In 3x + 7, the constant is 7
Guess and Check
A strategy where you guess a value, test it in the equation, and adjust based on the result.
Try x = 5 in 2x + 3 = 13: 2(5)+3=13 ✓

🔢 3.3 — Solving Two-Step Equations

A two-step equation has two operations applied to the variable. To solve it, you undo those operations in reverse order — like unwrapping a present, outer layer first!

2x + 5 = 13   means   "x is multiplied by 2, then 5 is added"

🔑 The Strategy: Work Backwards (Reverse PEMDAS)

When solving, undo operations in this order:

Step 1: Undo any addition or subtraction (move constants to the other side)

Step 2: Undo any multiplication or division (isolate the variable)

📋 Step-by-Step Example 1

Solve:   2x + 5 = 13
  • 1
    Undo addition: Subtract 5 from both sides
    2x + 5 − 5 = 13 − 5  →  2x = 8
  • 2
    Undo multiplication: Divide both sides by 2
    2x ÷ 2 = 8 ÷ 2  →  x = 4
  • 3
    Check: 2(4) + 5 = 8 + 5 = 13 ✓

📋 Step-by-Step Example 2

Solve:   3x − 4 = 17
  • 1
    Undo subtraction: Add 4 to both sides
    3x − 4 + 4 = 17 + 4  →  3x = 21
  • 2
    Undo multiplication: Divide both sides by 3
    3x ÷ 3 = 21 ÷ 3  →  x = 7
  • 3
    Check: 3(7) − 4 = 21 − 4 = 17 ✓

📋 Division Version

Solve:   x/3 + 2 = 8
  • 1
    Undo addition: Subtract 2 from both sides → x/3 = 6
  • 2
    Undo division: Multiply both sides by 3 → x = 18
  • 3
    Check: 18/3 + 2 = 6 + 2 = 8 ✓

🌍 Real-Life Example — Cell Phone Plan

A cell phone plan charges $10 per month plus $0.05 per text message. Terria's bill was $17.50 this month. How many texts did she send?

Let t = number of texts.
Equation: 0.05t + 10 = 17.50
Step 1: Subtract 10 → 0.05t = 7.50
Step 2: Divide by 0.05 → t = 150 texts
Check: 0.05(150) + 10 = 7.50 + 10 = 17.50 ✓

🌍 Real-Life Example — Saving for Sneakers

Terria already has $14 saved. She earns $8 per hour working at a community program. She needs $62 total for new sneakers. How many hours must she work?

Let h = hours worked.
Equation: 8h + 14 = 62
Step 1: Subtract 14 → 8h = 48
Step 2: Divide by 8 → h = 6 hours
Check: 8(6) + 14 = 48 + 14 = 62 ✓

✂️ 3.4 — Simplifying Before Solving

Sometimes equations look more complicated than they really are! Before solving, we need to simplify by combining like terms or using the distributive property.

Part A: Combining Like Terms

If the same variable appears more than once on the same side, combine them first!

Solve:   4x + 2x − 3 = 21
  • 1
    Combine like terms: 4x + 2x = 6x
    Equation becomes: 6x − 3 = 21
  • 2
    Undo subtraction: Add 3 to both sides → 6x = 24
  • 3
    Undo multiplication: Divide by 6 → x = 4
  • 4
    Check: 4(4) + 2(4) − 3 = 16 + 8 − 3 = 21 ✓

Part B: Distributive Property

If you see parentheses, distribute FIRST, then solve!

Solve:   3(x + 5) = 27
  • 1
    Distribute: 3(x) + 3(5) = 27
    Equation becomes: 3x + 15 = 27
  • 2
    Subtract 15 from both sides: 3x = 12
  • 3
    Divide by 3: x = 4
  • 4
    Check: 3(4 + 5) = 3(9) = 27 ✓

Part C: Both! (Distribute AND Combine)

Solve:   2(x + 3) + 4x = 30
  • 1
    Distribute: 2x + 6 + 4x = 30
  • 2
    Combine like terms: 6x + 6 = 30
  • 3
    Subtract 6: 6x = 24
  • 4
    Divide by 6: x = 4
  • 5
    Check: 2(4+3) + 4(4) = 14 + 16 = 30 ✓

🌍 Real-Life Example — Class Party

For a school party, each table needs 3 cups and 3 plates. There are also 5 extra cups for teachers. All together, there are 29 items. How many tables are there?

Let t = number of tables.
Each table: 3 + 3 = 6 items → Equation: 6t + 5 = 29
Step 1: Subtract 5 → 6t = 24
Step 2: Divide by 6 → t = 4 tables
Check: 6(4) + 5 = 24 + 5 = 29 ✓

🌍 Real-Life Example — Buying Snacks

Terria and 2 friends each buy the same snack combo. They also split the cost of a shared dessert that costs $6. The total bill is $30. What did each snack combo cost?

Let c = cost of each combo.
Equation: 3c + 6 = 30
Step 1: Subtract 6 → 3c = 24
Step 2: Divide by 3 → c = $8 per combo
Check: 3(8) + 6 = 24 + 6 = 30 ✓
