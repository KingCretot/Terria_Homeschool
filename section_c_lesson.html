📚 Key Vocabulary — Section C

Mathematical Model
An equation that represents a real-world situation using math.
"You earn $12 per hour" → 12h = total pay
Define the Variable
To clearly state what the variable represents, usually written as "Let ___ = ___".
Let h = number of hours worked
Inequality
A mathematical statement showing that two expressions are NOT equal, using <, >, ≤, ≥, or ≠.
x > 5 means "x is greater than 5"
Solution Set
All values that make an inequality true — there are infinitely many solutions!
x > 3: solutions are 4, 5, 6, 7... (and 3.1, 3.5, etc.)
Open Circle
Used on a number line when the endpoint is NOT included (< or >).
x > 4 → open circle at 4
Closed Circle
Used on a number line when the endpoint IS included (≤ or ≥).
x ≥ 4 → closed circle at 4
Solving an Inequality
Same steps as solving an equation EXCEPT: when multiplying or dividing by a NEGATIVE, flip the inequality sign.
−2x < 8 → divide by −2 → x > −4 (sign flips!)
Inequality Symbols
< less than  |  > greater than  |  ≤ less than OR equal to  |  ≥ greater than OR equal to
x ≤ 10 means x can be 10, 9, 8... or any number below

🌍 3.5 — Using Equations to Model Real-World Problems

Equations are powerful tools! In Section 3.5, we take real-life situations and translate them into equations, then solve. This is one of the most important skills in all of math.

🔑 The 4-Step Problem-Solving Process

  • 1
    Define the variable — write "Let x = ___"
  • 2
    Write the equation — translate words into math symbols
  • 3
    Solve the equation — use your skills from 3.1–3.4
  • 4
    Answer in context — state what the answer means in the real world

📖 Key Translation Words

Math OperationWords That Signal It
Addition (+)plus, sum, more than, increased by, total, altogether
Subtraction (−)minus, difference, less than, decreased by, fewer, remain
Multiplication (×)times, product, of, per, doubled, tripled, each
Division (÷)divided by, quotient, per, split equally, shared
Equals (=)is, equals, gives, results in, totals, was

🌍 Example 1 — Car Wash Fundraiser

The student council charges $8 per car for a fundraiser. They need to raise $200 for a trip. How many cars must they wash?

Step 1: Let c = number of cars
Step 2: Equation: 8c = 200
Step 3: Divide both sides by 8 → c = 25
Step 4: They must wash 25 cars.

🌍 Example 2 — Phone Data Plan

A data plan costs $30 per month plus $0.10 per gigabyte (GB) used. Terria's bill was $43. How many GB did she use?

Step 1: Let g = gigabytes used
Step 2: Equation: 0.10g + 30 = 43
Step 3: Subtract 30 → 0.10g = 13; Divide by 0.10 → g = 130
Step 4: She used 130 GB.

🌍 Example 3 — Geometry Connection

The perimeter of a rectangle is 42 cm. The length is 13 cm. What is the width?

Formula: P = 2l + 2w
Step 1: Let w = width
Step 2: Equation: 2(13) + 2w = 42 → 26 + 2w = 42
Step 3: Subtract 26 → 2w = 16; Divide by 2 → w = 8
Step 4: The width is 8 cm.

⚖️ 3.6 — Solving Inequalities

An inequality is like an equation, but instead of saying two things are equal, it says one is greater than or less than the other. The amazing thing? Inequalities have infinitely many solutions!

x + 3 > 7   means   "some number plus 3 is greater than 7"
Solutions: x = 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12 ... (and 4.1, 4.5, etc.!)

📊 The Four Inequality Symbols

SymbolMeaningExampleNumber Line
<less thanx < 5Open circle at 5, arrow pointing LEFT ←
>greater thanx > 3Open circle at 3, arrow pointing RIGHT →
≤less than or equal tox ≤ 5CLOSED circle at 5, arrow pointing LEFT ←
≥greater than or equal tox ≥ 3CLOSED circle at 3, arrow pointing RIGHT →

🔑 How to Solve an Inequality

SAME steps as solving an equation! EXCEPT one special rule:

⚠️ FLIP THE SIGN: When you multiply or divide BOTH sides by a NEGATIVE number, you must reverse the inequality symbol.
−2x < 8  →  divide both sides by −2  →  x > −4  (sign FLIPPED!)

📋 Example 1: Simple Inequality

Solve and graph:   x + 4 < 9
  • 1
    Subtract 4 from both sides: x < 5
  • 2
    Graph: Open circle at 5, arrow pointing LEFT (all numbers less than 5)
  • 3
    Check with x = 3: 3 + 4 = 7 < 9 ✓  |  Check with x = 6: 6 + 4 = 10 NOT < 9 (correct, 6 should NOT be in solution)

↑ Number line showing x < 5 (open circle, arrow pointing left)

📋 Example 2: Two-Step Inequality

Solve and graph:   2x − 3 ≥ 7
  • 1
    Add 3 to both sides: 2x ≥ 10
  • 2
    Divide by 2: x ≥ 5
  • 3
    Graph: Closed circle at 5, arrow pointing RIGHT (all numbers 5 and greater)
  • 4
    Check with x = 5: 2(5)−3 = 7 ≥ 7 ✓  |  Check with x = 8: 2(8)−3 = 13 ≥ 7 ✓

↑ Closed circle at 5, arrow pointing right (x ≥ 5)

🌍 Real-Life Example — Height Requirement

You must be at least 48 inches tall to ride an amusement park ride. Terria is currently 44 inches. How many more inches must she grow?

Let g = inches to grow.
Inequality: 44 + g ≥ 48
Solve: g ≥ 4
Terria must grow at least 4 more inches.
